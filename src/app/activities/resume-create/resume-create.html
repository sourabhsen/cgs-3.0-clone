How do you tailor your resume for a specific job?

You want to make it as easy as possible for a hiring manager to quickly see why YOU are the right person for the job. This means that you should tailor your resume for EVERY job that you apply to.

  • Print out the job description or paste it into the "notes" section of our Resume Builder tool
  • Read through the job description carefully. (You should fully understand what this job entails.)
  • Highlight the key parts of the job description
  • Make sure that the skills and experience you list are the ones the outlined in the job description. (Just make sure that you only add skills and experience that you actually have.)

What are the most important parts of the job description?

Job descriptions come in all shapes and sizes, however most have very similar sections. When going through and highlighting the important parts of the job posting, read every word.

  • Read EVERY word - It's easy to miss a key requirement like "expert with Excel" buried in a long list of generic requirements.
  • Dismiss the fluff - You'll see general requirements like "strong verbal communication skills" in a lot of job descriptions. This is important to keep in mind, however you want to focus on the requirements that are specific to this job.
  • Focus on the job requirements - Is a Bachelor degree required? Are certain certifications required? Do you need to be an expert with a certain type of software? How many years of experience do they want you to have? These are the items that you want to make sure your resume addresses.
  • Refer to the job duties - Read between the lines. If they say that you will spend 8 hours a day on the phone with customers, those "strong verbal communications skills" are probably very important.
  • Pay attention to the skills - Highlight the important skills so you can include the ones you have in the skills section of your resume

Remember, hiring managers often receive hundreds of resumes for a single position. They should read your resume and realize that you are a great match. When you finish your resume, re-read the job description and then read your resume. Would you hire yourself?
